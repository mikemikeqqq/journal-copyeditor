# Chinese-Influenced English Diagnostic

Use this reference when polishing English manuscripts written by Chinese-speaking authors or translated from Chinese notes.

## Editing Stance

Translate intent, not syntax. Preserve the author's scientific meaning, but reshape sentences into natural academic English.

## Common Patterns

### Article and Count-Noun Errors

Signals:

- missing `a`, `an`, or `the` before singular count nouns;
- unnecessary `the` before general concepts;
- plural/singular inconsistency.

Fix when meaning is clear. Do not comment unless the noun choice changes meaning.

### Overlong Sentence Chains

Signals:

- one sentence contains background, method, result, and implication;
- repeated `which`, `and`, `while`, `therefore`;
- sentence order follows Chinese explanatory sequence rather than English reader sequence.

Repair:

1. split claim, evidence, condition, comparison, implication, and limitation;
2. put the main claim early;
3. move supporting details after the claim.

### Obvious / Significant Without Comparator

Signals:

- `obvious`, `obviously`, `significant`, `significantly improved`, `remarkable`;
- no baseline, comparator, measure, or statistical test.

Repair:

- add comparator if supplied;
- otherwise soften and flag in the report.

### First / Novel Without Scope

Signals:

- `first`, `novel`, `innovative`, `pioneering`;
- no field, population, dataset, region, method, or time scope.

Repair:

- add scope when supplied: `to our knowledge, this is the first study in...`;
- otherwise comment or report that novelty needs verification.

### Results and Discussion Mixed

Signals:

- Results paragraphs include long explanations of mechanism or significance;
- Discussion repeats numeric results without interpretation;
- implications appear before observations are clear.

Repair:

- Results: what was observed, under what conditions, with what support.
- Discussion: what it means, how it relates to prior work, and where it may not apply.

### Literal Translation Markers

Watch for:

- `with the development of society/science`;
- `people pay more and more attention to`;
- `has important theoretical and practical significance`;
- `the research on X is still in its infancy`;
- `at home and abroad`;
- `make a deep analysis of`.

Replace with concrete field context, research gap, or contribution.

## Report Triggers

Include an issue in the final report when:

- the English can be polished, but the intended claim remains ambiguous;
- a Chinese-style expression hides a missing comparator, citation, or scope;
- terminology choices affect constructs, variables, methods, or theory;
- novelty or significance wording may attract reviewer challenge.

## Comment Examples

- `Please confirm the comparator for "significantly improved."`
- `Please verify whether this is intended as a causal claim or an association.`
- `Please confirm the scope of "first" here, such as population, setting, or method.`
