# Copyediting Contract

Use this reference to choose the editing depth and decide whether to edit, comment, or leave material unchanged.

## Editing Levels

Default to `medium` unless the user specifies another level.

| Level | Use for | Allowed changes |
|---|---|---|
| `light` | Near-final manuscripts, journal resubmissions, or sensitive author voice | Grammar, punctuation, article use, word order, typo fixes, obvious concision |
| `medium` | Most journal manuscripts by multilingual authors | Light edits plus sentence restructuring, transitions, paragraph openings, hedging, terminology consistency |
| `heavy` | Rough drafts or explicitly requested deep polishing | Medium edits plus paragraph reordering, repeated-concept consolidation, section-level flow repair, comment-backed claim revisions |

Never let editing depth override factual fidelity. Heavy copyediting is still copyediting, not ghostwriting a new scientific argument.

Even in `heavy` mode, tracked changes should be as local as practical. Whole-paragraph replacement requires a real paragraph-level reason and should be mentioned in the report.

## Edit, Comment, or Preserve

Edit directly when:

- grammar, punctuation, article use, or sentence order is clearly wrong;
- a sentence is wordy but meaning is stable;
- terminology can be made consistent without changing scope;
- a claim needs routine hedging that does not change the result.

Use the smallest possible direct edit. For example, replace one phrase rather than the whole sentence when the rest of the sentence is already usable.

Add an editor comment when:

- the intended meaning is ambiguous;
- a causal, mechanistic, novelty, or generalization claim appears unsupported;
- changing the wording may alter the theory, interpretation, or contribution;
- a statistic, citation, figure/table callout, abbreviation, or term appears inconsistent;
- a citation cluster or reference entry appears wrong but is embedded in a citation-manager field that cannot be safely edited;
- a paragraph contains two possible messages and the author must choose one.

When the requested editor identity is `Catherine`, new decision comments must be authored as `Catherine`, and the final tracked DOCX must preserve those comments.

Preserve or touch minimally when:

- the text is a direct quotation;
- the passage contains equations, code, field codes, reference entries, legal/ethics statements, funding statements, or author affiliations;
- citations are managed by Zotero, EndNote, Mendeley, Word bibliography fields, or another field system that may break if manually edited;
- a table, figure legend, or methods detail is precise but stylistically plain;
- editing would require domain knowledge not present in the manuscript.

## Author Voice and Style Samples

If the user supplies prior writing samples, treat them as a soft profile:

- preserve preferred hedging, transition style, and sentence rhythm when compatible with journal style;
- do not reproduce idiosyncratic grammar errors;
- discipline and target-journal conventions override personal style;
- notify the user only once if a visible style conflict matters.

## Query Style

Editor comments should be short, specific, and actionable. Prefer:

- `Please confirm whether this is a causal claim or an association.`
- `Please define this abbreviation at first use.`
- `This sentence appears to repeat the previous claim; please confirm whether a distinct point is intended.`
- `Please check whether the cited source supports this stronger novelty claim.`

Avoid comments that merely describe your edit, praise the prose, or ask the author to "review" without naming the decision.

## Submission-Safe Boundaries

- Do not fabricate references, line numbers, data, analyses, software versions, accession numbers, or reporting-guideline compliance.
- Do not silently upgrade association to causation.
- Do not remove limitations to make the contribution sound stronger.
- Do not weaken a valid technical claim only because the language is complex.
- Do not standardize spelling across quoted titles or reference entries unless requested.
- Do not silently fix uncertain citation/reference problems. Add them to the in-document `Copy Editing and Proofreading Report`, and comment in the manuscript when the author must decide.
- Do not turn ordinary copyediting into a full-paragraph delete/reinsert tracked change. The author must be able to see the exact local changes.
- Do not remove, resolve, or lose Catherine comments in the tracked-copy deliverable.
